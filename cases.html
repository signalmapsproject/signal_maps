Cases.

These are the project’s core case studies: places where the broader method is tested against a concrete event, proxy signal, or interpretive split. Each page is built to separate what is directly visible, what can be inferred, what rival explanations remain alive, and what still cannot be resolved from the public record.

Use the cases for close reading, not as shortcuts around uncertainty.

How to use this page

Start with the featured cases, then use the reading notes to keep the standard of proof steady from page to page. This index is meant to route the reader into disciplined reading, not replace it.

Use the cases to see how the method behaves under pressure. The goal is not a dramatic verdict. It is a clearer, slower reading of what the record can and cannot support.

02 · Reading notes

How to read the cases

These notes keep the case pages aligned with the method instead of letting one dramatic signal quietly become the whole standard of proof.

What to look for

  • What is directly visible versus what is inferred.
  • Where a signal appears first and how it moves.
  • Whether a proxy is suggestive, strong, or near-direct.
  • Which rival explanations still remain alive.

What not to assume

  • That every silence indicates suppression.
  • That a non-Western source is automatically more truthful.
  • That a compelling proxy settles motive or attribution.
  • That disagreement alone means deception.
