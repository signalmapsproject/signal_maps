Method · Working framework · Version 3

The Exoplanet Method.

This project studies contested information environments by treating visibility, delay, routing, silence, and proxy indicators as patterns that can be investigated. The method does not assume that every gap is suppression. It asks whether the gap comes from control, ordinary noise, source ecology, timing, or the analyst’s own search limits.

The public version below is the readable site framework. In practice, it connects directly to the workbook, the Signal Ledger, the case pages, and the workflow relay used to keep uncertainty visible instead of smoothing it away.

Governing rule: absence should initiate investigation, not terminate it.

Reading frame

This method is built for structured uncertainty. It is meant to help map asymmetry in information flow, test rival explanations, grade proxy evidence, and keep the analyst honest about what is solid, what is probable, and what remains unresolved.

Absence is not proof. It is a lead.

01
Core rules

Start with what is visible

Before chasing gaps, map what is directly observable: who is carrying the story, which facts repeat across unrelated systems, and which evidence appears first.

Classify absence before interpreting it

An absence can be structural, incidental, or observer-created. It also needs a basis: documented policy, historical pattern, comparative evidence, or analyst inference.

Track where the signal went

Absence is directional, not binary. A signal missing in one sphere but visible in another is a routing clue. It is not automatically proof.

Timing and persistence matter

Log when the event appears, when attribution appears, and whether the story is sustained or allowed to die. Coverage half-life can be as revealing as first mention.

Rival explanations are required

Do not jump from pattern to verdict. For important inferences, test competing explanations such as suppression, incompetence, verification failure, strategic ambiguity, or narrative seeding.

The analyst is part of the dataset

Search terms, languages, platforms, assumptions, and time pressure affect what looks absent. The method audits the analyst as well as the sources.

02
Operational workflow
01
Pre-analysis stance. State the working hypothesis, what would disconfirm it, and any pressure or prior investment that may shape interpretation.
02
Visible map. Track which outlets, formats, and claims are present before interpreting what is missing.
03
Key absences. Identify what seems missing, then classify both the absence and the basis for the classification.
04
Directional routing. Ask where the signal appears instead, and what kind of source ecosystem is carrying it.
05
Temporal map. Log event time, first mention, first attribution, first visual evidence, and any disappearance from follow-up coverage.
06
Proxy indicators. Use hospital, transport, insurance, market, or infrastructure signals carefully and grade them from suggestive to near-direct.
07
Rival hypotheses. For each major inference, identify real supporting evidence for the alternatives as well as for the leading interpretation.
08
Epistemic labels. Separate verified facts, probable inferences, open hypotheses, narrative claims, and disputed claims.
09
Outside-view checkpoint. Ask what all major spheres may be missing because of shared assumptions or blind spots.
10
Human impact note. End by asking who is counted, who is invisible, and what remains unresolved about harm to civilians.
03
What this method is for
Strongest use

Structured uncertainty

The method is strongest as a way to map asymmetry in information flow, generate better investigative questions, and keep analysis honest about what is solid and what is still open.

Important boundary

Not a shortcut to proof

The method does not convert silence into causality and does not treat every omission as intentional suppression. It is a framework for disciplined inquiry, not a machine for dramatic certainty.

04
Limits and safeguards

Major risks

  • Overreading absence.
  • Mistaking different bias for truer bias.
  • Confusing search failure with suppression.
  • Treating planted trails as organic signals.
  • Letting rival-hypothesis testing become ritual rather than restraint.

Safeguards

  • Audit the search method.
  • Make the basis for classification explicit.
  • Grade the source sphere and the topic-specific reliability of the source.
  • Track story decay as well as first appearance.
  • Keep a clear boundary between fact, inference, and speculation.
